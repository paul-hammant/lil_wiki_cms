Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep? is a 1968 dystopian science fiction novel by American writer Philip K. Dick.

## Overview

Set in a post-apocalyptic San Francisco, the novel follows Rick Deckard, a bounty hunter tasked with "retiring" escaped androids that are virtually indistinguishable from humans. The novel was the basis for the film Blade Runner.

## Plot

In a world where most animals are extinct and owning a real animal is a status symbol, Deckard hunts Nexus-6 androids while questioning what truly separates humans from machines.

## AI Themes

- What defines humanity vs artificial beings
- Empathy as a distinguishing trait
- The ethics of creating beings for servitude
- Identity and authenticity
- The Voigt-Kampff test (inspiration for the Turing test in film)

## Legacy

The novel's exploration of consciousness and identity has made it a foundational text in discussions of AI ethics and what it means to be human.
